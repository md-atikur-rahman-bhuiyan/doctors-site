Every guy knows. Every ace who makes his way across my threshold knows that he is tardy – usually by months and often by years. And each of the brave ones knows that his priorities are out of whack when he shuffles into my office. How? There is a voice inside of him. I don’t know from where the voice emanates, but I know it’s there. I know it’s there because I was once there, in his tattered leather loafers. There are feelings that accompany the voice. The feelings go away in short stents if he pacifies the voice:
